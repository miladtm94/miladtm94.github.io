Research Vision & Interests

My research focuses on developing AI-native wireless systems for 6G and beyond, where communication, sensing, and security are co-designed as a unified and adaptive physical-layer intelligence. I build rigorous foundations grounded in information theory, estimation theory, and statistical signal processing, and translate them into scalable algorithms using a combination of optimization, learning, and data-driven modeling.

A central objective of my work is to characterize and exploit the fundamental trade-offs between reliability, latency, security, and environmental awareness in emerging networks. This includes advancing integrated sensing and communications (ISAC), reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (IRS), and non-terrestrial platforms (UAV/satellite), under realistic constraints such as finite blocklength operation, hardware impairments, and adversarial interference.

Looking ahead, I am particularly interested in learning-driven and self-evolving network architectures, where reinforcement learning, generative models, and foundation models are used in a principled manner to enable context-aware control, scalable resource allocation, and resilient system adaptation. A key direction is bridging model-based design with data-driven intelligence to achieve robust, interpretable, and deployable wireless systems.
